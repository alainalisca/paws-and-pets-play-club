01 / Walks

Dog walking

Fifteen or thirty minutes around your block. Water before we leave, real potty break, real playtime, treats if you've packed them — and a couple of photos sent before I'm back at your door. I keep walks within Washingtonville so your dog's walk doesn't get eaten by drive time.

Can I book a recurring schedule?
Yes — weekly recurring clients save 10%. Set a regular day/time and I'll lock it in. Easier on me, easier on you, easier on your dog.
What if it's raining or icy?
We still go — dogs need their walk. I dress for it. If conditions are genuinely unsafe (lightning, ice), we shorten the walk or do a backyard potty break and indoor playtime instead.
Do you walk multiple dogs at once?
Yes, if they live in the same household and get along. Add $5 per additional dog. I don't combine dogs from different households on the same walk.

Where does day care happen?
Either your home (most common — pets prefer their own space) or my home, where my own pets JJ and Bél live. We'll talk through what's best for your pet at the meet & greet.
How far do you travel for day care?
Up to a 20-minute drive from Washingtonville — Salisbury Mills, Blooming Grove, and surrounding towns within that radius. Anything further, ask me.
Can I drop my pet off and pick up?
Yes — drop-off at my home is welcome. We'll set drop-off and pick-up windows that work for your day.

03 / Overnights

Overnight care

I stay with your pet so they don't sleep alone. Beds get used, schedules get kept, evening walks happen, breakfast is on time. Photos in the morning so you wake up to good news. I'll travel further for overnights than for walks — let's talk distance.

Where do you stay overnight?
In your home, with your pet. They sleep where they normally sleep. The whole point is to keep their routine intact while you're away.
How far do you travel for overnights?
Cornwall, Goshen, Monroe, Chester, and beyond. If you're vacationing further out, ask — I might still be able to make it work.
What about while I'm at school during the day?
For overnights, my mom or dad covers the daytime gap when I'm in class. Your pet is never alone for long stretches.

Cat care

Feeding, fresh water, scoop the box, real playtime, and the photo update I promise on every service. Cats are not afterthoughts — Bél lives with me and she's taught me what indifference looks like, what affection looks like, and what to do with the in-between.

Will you give medications?
Yes — pills, drops, topical, subcutaneous insulin. Tell me at the meet & greet so we can practice if needed. Bring written instructions from your vet.
My cat is shy. Will that be a problem?
Not at all. I'd rather sit on the floor reading a book in your cat's eyeline than chase them around. Comfort first; trust grows on its own schedule.

Small animals & fish

Hamsters, guinea pigs, rabbits, fish — I've cared for all of them. Cage cleaning, fresh hay and bedding, tank checks, water level and temperature, hands-on time so social animals don't get lonely. Pricing varies with the setup, so text me with what you've got and I'll quote.

Reptiles? Birds?
I'll take birds for sure. Reptiles depending on the species and care needs — text me and let's talk.
What's the typical price range?
Most small-animal visits land between $10 and $20 per visit, depending on whether it's a quick feeding-and-check or a full cage clean. I'll quote you exactly before we start.

Good to know

Before we start.

A short list so we're aligned from day one. Nothing here is unusual — it's how every careful sitter works.

  1. 01

    Free meet & greet

    Required before your first booking. Pets and people both need a hello before anyone hands over a leash.

  2. 02

    48 hours notice

    Book at least two days out when possible. Cancellations: same 48 hours for a full refund.

  3. 03

    Vaccinations up to date

    For the safety of every pet I care for, including yours.

  4. 04

    You provide supplies

    Food, treats, medications, leashes, anything pet-specific. I bring me — you bring their stuff.

  5. 05

    Trimmed nails

    Safer handling and play, fewer accidental scratches for everyone.

  6. 06

    Friendly pets only

    I don't take aggressive animals. Most everything else — anxious, shy, senior, energetic — is welcome.
