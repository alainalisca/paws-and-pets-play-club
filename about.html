Meet your pet sitter

Hi, I'm Alix.

Sixteen, born and raised in Washingtonville. I've been the kid the neighborhood asks to watch their dog since I was old enough to walk one.

I started watching dogs the way every kid starts a thing — by saying yes to the first person who asked. A neighbor, a school friend's family, a parent's coworker. Eight years in, it's how my afternoons and weekends are spent. The Paws and Pets Play Club is what I call it now — but the work has been the same since I was eight: show up, pay attention, treat the pet like family.

I do this with my parents' full backing. When I'm at school, sports, or theater rehearsal, my mom or dad steps in so your pet is never alone. That's the deal we made before I started taking on overnights. It's also the reason I can say yes to longer stays when other teen sitters can't — the family's covering me when I can't be there.

JJ, my Chihuahua, and Bél, my cat, live with me. They've taught me everything that matters about caring for somebody else's pet. JJ is opinionated, has a sweater for every weather, and refuses walks under thirty-eight degrees. Bél tolerates everyone and prefers nobody. Between them, I've learned what indifference looks like, what affection looks like, and what to do with the in-between.

Family-supported

Never solo. Always answered.

I'm sixteen. That means school, sports, theater. It also means I'm not the only person responsible when your pet is in my care. My parents back this up — when I'm in class or on stage, one of them is on call to step in. Day-of pickups, midday let-outs during overnights, an extra walk when the schedule slides — they're part of how this works.

Every client meets us as a family. We tell you exactly who's covering what, when. That way, when I'm in physics class and you text from the airport, you know somebody's already at your house.

How it usually goes

A day in the life.

A typical Saturday during peak season looks something like this.

7:15 AM

Bél's breakfast

My own cat first. Wet food, fresh water, brief argument about which counter is hers.

8:00 AM

Morning walks

Two regulars in Washingtonville, back-to-back thirty-minute walks. Photos sent before I'm home for breakfast.

11:30 AM

Day-care drop in

A doodle in Salisbury Mills whose family is at a wedding. Lunch, walk, fetch in the yard, photos.

2:00 PM

Theater rehearsal

My mom takes the next walk. She texts the client and the dog's family before she leaves the house.

5:30 PM

Meet & greet

A new client in Cornwall, free first visit. We sit on the floor with the dog. Twenty minutes, no pressure.

8:00 PM

Overnight sit

Drive to Goshen, stay with a senior beagle whose owner is on a long weekend. Last walk before bed, and morning starts again.

"Future plan: sports physical therapy. Right now: making sure your dog gets a real walk and your cat gets a real scratch behind the ears."
